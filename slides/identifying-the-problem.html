What do you think about typing? Is it fun? Do you like to type? Is it a pain? Is it a necessary evil?

Why Do We Type?

  • Get specific information into a computer
It's very simple we do it to get very specific information into a computer.

Typing Is Not The Most Direct Way To Express Your Thoughts

  • Misspellings
  • Typos
Typing is not the most direct way to express your thoughts. Because of our human nature we make mistakes and in the context of typing those mistakes usually manifest as misspellings and typos. So what can we do about these problems?

Smartphones Have Assistive Keyboard Applications

  • Auto-correction
  • Auto-completion
  • Word-suggestion
Take a look at your smart phone. It has a keyboard application which auto-corrects your misspelled words and typos. The fancier keyboard apps even complete your words and insert correct punctuation. The fanciest keyboard apps suggest words to use next based on your previous input, meaning that it's context aware. If you're writing in the past tense word suggestion are also in the past tense. In addition to all these useful features all keyboard apps on smartphones work on every application on your device. The typing experience you had on one app is exactly the same as you have on another app. So what about Desktops?

Desktops Have Not Standardized The Typing Experience

  • Individual applications may have assistive typing
  • No guarantee your typing experience will be the same across applications
Desktops have fallen behind mobile devices when it comes to providing tools for more efficient typing experiences. Think of your typing experience in MS Word vs Notepad. It's a drastically different experience. MS Word provides some correction capabilities, sentence analysis, etc. Notepad on the other hand provides none of those features. The desktop environment needs a tool which will standardize the typing experience no matter if your browsing the web or jotting down some notes. Unfortunately I know of no tools on the desktop which provide this experience.

Problem Summary

There is no known tool which standardizes the typing experience on the desktop while providing basic assistive typing features like auto-correction and auto-completion.
